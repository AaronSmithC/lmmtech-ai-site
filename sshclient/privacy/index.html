$ In one line: SSHClient is built to collect nothing.
No accounts. No analytics. No tracking. No cloud sync. The only network connection the app makes is the SSH session you start, directly to the server you choose.

Who this covers

This policy applies to the iPhone application SSHClient – Markdown ("the app"), published by LMM Technologies, Inc. ("we," "us"). It explains what the app does and does not do with information when you use it.

Information we collect

We do not collect personal information. The app has no user accounts, no sign-up, and no login to any service operated by us. We do not operate servers that receive your usage data, and we do not use third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking software in the app.

Information stored on your device

To do its job, the app stores certain information locally on your iPhone. This data stays on your device and is not transmitted to us:

  • Saved hosts. The connection details you choose to save (such as hostname, port, and username) are stored locally on your device so you can reconnect quickly.
  • Passwords. If you save a password for a host, it is stored in the iOS Keychain, the system's encrypted credential store. Passwords are never stored in plain text and never leave your device through the app.
  • Host keys. The app pins each server's host key on first connection (trust-on-first-use) and stores it locally to warn you if a server's key later changes.
  • Session transcripts. When you choose to copy or save a transcript, that action uses your device's clipboard or storage. The app does not retain or transmit transcripts on its own.

The SSH connection

When you connect to a server, the app opens an SSH connection directly from your device to the server you specify. The contents of that session travel between your phone and your server. They are not routed through, proxied by, or stored on any infrastructure operated by LMM Technologies. What happens on the servers you connect to is governed by those servers and their operators, not by this app.

Network and local discovery

The app can resolve .local / Bonjour hostnames to help you reach machines on your local network. This discovery happens on your device and your local network; it is not reported to us.

Children's privacy

The app is a developer tool and is not directed to children under 13. We do not knowingly collect information from children.

Data sharing and sale

We do not sell, rent, or share your information, because the app does not collect it. There are no third parties receiving data from the app.

Your control

Because data is stored locally, you remain in control of it. You can remove saved hosts within the app, and you can delete the app to remove its locally stored data from your device, including credentials held in the iOS Keychain for the app.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. When we make material changes, we will update the date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after an update means you accept the revised policy.
