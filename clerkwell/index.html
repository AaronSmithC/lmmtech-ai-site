How it works

Three steps. No prompts to learn.

The whole tool is a screen of labeled buttons, set up for your trade. Anyone on staff can use it on day one — if they can fill in a form, they can use Clerkwell.

1

Pick a labeled button

“Past-due reminder,” “Appointment notice,” “Estimate cover letter” — the buttons match the work you actually do.

2

Fill in a few blanks

A short form asks for the names, dates, and amounts. Jot rough notes — Clerkwell shapes them into proper writing.

3

Review, then use it

A clean draft appears in seconds. Edit anything, then copy or print. A person always approves before it goes out.

Why Clerkwell

Quietly capable. Deliberately bounded.

● Nothing leaves the building

The model runs on a box in your office. Client names, balances, records — none of it touches the cloud. For confidentiality-bound offices, that's the whole point.

● Paid once, not monthly

Buy the box, own the tool. No per-seat subscription, no usage meter, no surprise bill — built for offices that are done renting their software.

● Safe by design

Clerkwell writes; it never does your math or pretends to be your records. The one rule that makes it safe to drop into a tax office, a clinic, and a funeral home alike.

● Nothing to learn

No prompts, no AI literacy required. Labeled buttons and short forms — the same muscle memory as any paper form your staff already fills out.

The name. A smith is a maker — one who forges raw iron into something that works. A clerk writes and files, but never decides or computes. Clerkwell forges your rough notes into a finished draft, and the “-well” is the maker's mark: done well.
